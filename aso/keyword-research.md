## Executive Summary

The subscription tracker niche is **actively growing** with strong organic demand, driven by subscription fatigue (avg US adult has 4–6 paid subscriptions). The market splits into two segments: (1) heavyweight bank-linked budgeting apps (Rocket Money, PocketGuard, Mint) and (2) lightweight privacy-first trackers (Bobby, Subscriptions, SubTracky). The indie segment is **competitive but beatable** — most indie apps have < 5K reviews, ratings between 4.1–4.7, and clear UX gaps. Primary opportunity lies in **trial/free trial reminder keywords** (low competition, targeted intent) and **privacy-first positioning**.

### Competitive Insight Summary

- **Trial reminder niche is essentially empty.** Trial Alert has 1 review and 3 stars — this is a textbook opportunity.
- **Solo indie devs dominate the top of this niche** — no FAANG/corp lockout like expense tracking.
- **Review counts are low across the board** — SubTracky leads indie with only 172 reviews. 500+ reviews could make you #1.
- **Privacy-first positioning is a recurring theme** — all top indie apps emphasize "no bank link required." This is table stakes, not differentiation anymore.
- **iOS-only is the default** — most top apps are iOS-only, so cross-platform (iOS + Android) is underserved.

## Strategic Recommendation

**Best path: Build Idea 1 (Drip) first, use Idea 3 (TrialStop) as ASO trojan horse.**

Rationale:
- Idea 1 covers both the high-volume TIER 2-3 core keywords AND the TIER 1 trial keywords in one product.
- Lead the App Store listing with "trial reminder" and "free trial tracker" in the app title/subtitle to capture the low-competition TIER 1 traffic.
- As app power grows, keywords like "subscription manager" and "subscription tracker" become more achievable.
- If resources allow, Idea 3 (TrialStop) as a separate, lean app targeting exclusively TIER 1 keywords is a low-effort high-ROI move.

**Title formula**: `[App Name]: Subscription & Trial Tracker`
**Subtitle formula**: `Free Trial Reminder + Bill Manager`

This combination targets 6 of the highest-opportunity keywords in a single metadata field.
